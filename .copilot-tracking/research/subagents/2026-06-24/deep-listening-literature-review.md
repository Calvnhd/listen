### 1. BOOKS

1. [V] Pauline Oliveros — "Deep Listening: A Composer's Sound Practice" (2005, iUniverse). The foundational text; Oliveros coined "Deep Listening" (1988). Practice of attending to the entire field of sound (continuous awareness) vs. focused listening; includes meditations/exercises. ISBN 978-0-595-34365-2.
2. [V] R. Murray Schafer — "The Soundscape: Our Sonic Environment and the Tuning of the World" (1994 reprint of "The Tuning of the World," 1977, Knopf). Founding text of acoustic ecology/soundscape studies; keynote sounds, signals, soundmarks; ear cleaning; schizophonia. ISBN 978-0-89281-455-8.
3. [V] R. Murray Schafer — "A Sound Education: 100 Exercises in Listening and Soundmaking" (1992). Practical listening exercises — directly usable as session prompts.
4. [V] Michel Chion — "Audio-Vision: Sound on Screen" (Columbia UP, 1994; trans. Claudia Gorbman; orig. 1990). Film/recorded-sound listening; the three listening modes (causal, semantic, reduced); added value of sound to image. ISBN 0-231-07898-6.
5. [K] Eric F. Clarke — "Ways of Listening: An Ecological Approach to the Perception of Musical Meaning" (Oxford UP, 2005). Applies Gibsonian ecological perception to music listening; what sound "affords" the listener. URL: global.oup.com (ISBN 9780195151947). [page failed extraction this session]
6. [K] Aaron Copland — "What to Listen for in Music" (1939, McGraw-Hill). Classic music-appreciation primer; the "three planes" of listening (sensuous, expressive, sheerly musical).
7. [V] Daniel J. Levitin — "This Is Your Brain on Music" (Dutton, 2006). Accessible neuroscience of musical listening, expectation, emotion, timbre/rhythm/pitch.
8. [K] Elizabeth Hellmuth Margulis — "On Repeat: How Music Plays the Mind" (Oxford UP, 2014). How repetition shapes attention, enjoyment, and the experience of music (relevant to repeated/looped deep-listening sessions). URL: global.oup.com.
9. [V] David Huron — "Sweet Anticipation: Music and the Psychology of Expectation" (MIT Press, 2006). ITPRA theory; how expectation produces emotion (tension, prediction, chills).
10. [K] Jason Corey — "Audio Production and Critical Listening: Technical Ear Training" (Focal Press/Routledge; 1st ed. 2010, 2nd ed. 2017, with companion software). Systematic critical-listening training (timbre, EQ, dynamics, space). Most directly applicable to a "guided listening" tool.
11. [K] David Toop — "Ocean of Sound: Aether Talk, Ambient Sound and Imaginary Worlds" (Serpent's Tail, 1995). Poetic history of ambient/immersive listening from Debussy to Eno.
12. [K] David Toop — "Sinister Resonance: The Mediumship of the Listener" (Continuum, 2010). Listening as haunted, attentive, embodied act; sound's presence/absence.
13. [V] Pierre Schaeffer — "Treatise on Musical Objects: An Essay across Disciplines" (UC Press, 2017; trans. North & Dack; orig. 1966). Reduced listening, the "sound object," acousmatic listening, four listening modes. ISBN 9780520294301.
14. [V] Albert S. Bregman — "Auditory Scene Analysis: The Perceptual Organization of Sound" (MIT Press, 1990). How the brain parses overlapping sounds into streams — the science behind hearing layers in a soundscape.
15. [K] W. A. Mathieu — "The Listening Book: Discovering Your Own Music" (Shambhala, 1991). Gentle practical exercises in listening to everyday sound and music; very session-friendly.
16. [K] Joachim-Ernst Berendt — "The Third Ear: On Listening to the World" (1985/Eng. 1988). Listening as a path of awareness; world sound and music.
17. [K] Bernie Krause — "The Great Animal Orchestra" (Little, Brown, 2012). Soundscape ecology; biophony/geophony/anthrophony — framework for environmental listening.
18. [K] Gordon Hempton & John Grossmann — "One Square Inch of Silence" (Free Press, 2009). Quest to preserve natural quiet; listening to wild soundscapes.
19. [K] Pauline Oliveros — "Quantum Listening" (Ignota Books, 2022). Posthumous collection of her key essays on listening as practice/activism.
20. [K] Pauline Oliveros — "Sonic Meditations" (Smith Publications, 1974). Text-score listening/sounding meditations — direct source of guided-session material.
21. [K] Barry Truax — "Acoustic Communication" (Ablex, 1984; 2nd ed. 2001). Listening within a communicational model of sound/soundscape; companion to the WSP "Handbook for Acoustic Ecology."
22. [K] Salomé Voegelin — "Listening to Noise and Silence: Towards a Philosophy of Sound Art" (Continuum, 2010). Phenomenology of the listening experience.
23. [K] Don Ihde — "Listening and Voice: Phenomenologies of Sound" (2nd ed., SUNY, 2007; orig. 1976). Foundational phenomenology of auditory experience.
24. [K] Trevor Cox — "The Sound Book: The Science of the Sonic Wonders of the World" (Norton, 2014). Acoustic curiosities; trains curiosity-driven listening to environments.
25. [K] Jonathan Sterne (ed.) — "The Sound Studies Reader" (Routledge, 2012). Anthology contextualizing listening within sound studies.

### 2. ACADEMIC PAPERS & ARTICLES

1. [K] Patrik N. Juslin & Daniel Västfjäll — "Emotional responses to music: The need to consider underlying mechanisms," Behavioral and Brain Sciences 31(5), 2008. Introduces BRECVEM mechanisms (brain-stem reflex, rhythmic entrainment, evaluative conditioning, emotional contagion, visual imagery, episodic memory, musical expectancy). doi:10.1017/S0140525X08005293.
2. [K] Patrik N. Juslin — "From everyday emotions to aesthetic emotions: Towards a unified theory of musical emotions," Physics of Life Reviews 10(3), 2013. Extends model to BRECVEM-A (adds Aesthetic judgment). doi:10.1016/j.plrev.2013.05.008.
3. [K] Albert Bregman — see Auditory Scene Analysis (foundational for attentive listening to layered sound).
4. [K] Stephen Kaplan — "The restorative benefits of nature: Toward an integrative framework," Journal of Environmental Psychology 15(3), 1995. Attention Restoration Theory (ART); basis for restorative soundscapes. (See also R. & S. Kaplan, "The Experience of Nature," 1989.)
5. [K] Jian Kang et al. / ISO 12913-1:2014 "Acoustics — Soundscape — Part 1: Definitions and conceptual framework." Standardizes soundscape (perception of acoustic environment in context) — useful for wellbeing-oriented listening design.
6. [K] Pauline Oliveros — "Quantum Listening: From Practice to Theory (To Practice Practice)" (essay, 1999; widely circulated; collected in "Sounding the Margins," 2010, and "Quantum Listening," 2022).
7. [K] Pierre Schaeffer / Michel Chion — "Guide to Sound Objects: Pierre Schaeffer and Musical Research" (Chion, 1983; Eng. trans. by Dack & North, online via EARS/MTI). Practical gloss on reduced listening and typology of sound objects.
8. [K] Hildegard Westerkamp — "Soundwalking" (1974; revised in "Autumn Leaves," 2007). Method for guided attentive walking/listening — directly translatable to app sessions.
9. [K] R. Murray Schafer & World Soundscape Project — "The Vancouver Soundscape" / "Five Village Soundscapes" / "Handbook for Acoustic Ecology" (Truax, ed., 1978/1999). Primary acoustic-ecology source materials. URL: sfu.ca/~truax/wsp.html.
10. [K] Marvin Chion-adjacent film-sound scholarship — Walter Murch, "Dense Clarity – Clear Density" / writings on film listening (in "The Conversations," Ondaatje, 2002). Relevant to recorded/film-sound attention. [U] exact online source unverified.
11. [K] Ola Stockfelt — "Adequate Modes of Listening" (in "Audio Culture," eds. Cox & Warner, 2004). Theorizes listening modes/competences across contexts.
12. [K] Eric Clarke, Nicola Dibben, et al. — work on ecological/everyday music listening (e.g., Clarke, "Music, Space and Subjectivity"). Supports situated-listening session design.
